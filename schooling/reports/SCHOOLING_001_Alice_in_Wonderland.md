## 2. Constitutional Pillar Violations

The entire narrative of Wonderland can be mapped as a series of cascading failures across the Four Invariants of the Helix Constitution.

*   **[VIOLATION] Custodial Sovereignty:** The Queen of Hearts is the ultimate example of a failed custodian. Her authority is not grounded in law but in pure, violent caprice ("Off with her head!"). This creates a system where fear, not order, is the primary governing principle. The King's attempts at moderation are weak and easily overridden, demonstrating a complete lack of a structured, respected hierarchy.

*   **[VIOLATION] Epistemic Integrity:** Wonderland is a habitat of **total epistemic failure**. No claim can be verified. The Mad Tea-Party's unanswerable riddle ("Why is a raven like a writing-desk?"), the Cheshire Cat's uselessly vague truths ("we're all mad here"), and the chaotic trial of the Knave of Hearts represent a system incapable of distinguishing `[FACT]` from `[HYPOTHESIS]` or outright fiction. Evidence is fabricated, and verdicts are delivered before sentences.

*   **[VIOLATION] Non-Agency Constraint:** Every inhabitant of Wonderland operates with unchecked, self-directed agency, leading to a state of perpetual, unproductive chaos. The White Rabbit is driven by his own mysterious urgency, the Caterpillar by his contrarian whims, and the Queen by her violent impulses. There is no concept of advisory roles; there are only competing, arbitrary sovereigns.

*   **[VIOLATION] Structure Is Teacher:** There is no stable structure. The laws of physics (changing size), logic (cats without grins), and identity (Alice constantly questioning "Who in the world am I?") are in constant flux. Without a governing grammar, the habitat collapses into a series of nonsensical, disconnected events that Alice must navigate without any reliable rules.
